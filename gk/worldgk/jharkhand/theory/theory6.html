Rivers of Jharkhand and its origin

swarnarekha


It rises from a place called Rani Chuan in the village of Nagdi, located 16 km south-west of Ranchi city and leaves the main plateau, rising north-east and falls into the Bay of Bengal. ... The total length of this river is 474 km and it drains about 28928 sq km.

damodar


The Damodar River is a tributary of the Hooghly River. It flows in a west to east direction through Jharkhand and West Bengal. The river originates in the hills of the Chota Nagpur plateau of Jharkhand. The total length of the river is about 541 km, half of which lies in Jharkhand and half in West Bengal.

shankh


Shankh River is a river flowing in the states of Jharkhand, Chhattisgarh and Odisha, India. It originates at an altitude of 1,000 meters (3,300 ft) in the village of Lupungpat in Gumla district of Jharkhand. The Shankh river flows opposite the North Koel in the western end of the Chota Nagpur plateau.

north koel


The North Koel River is a river flowing in the state of Jharkhand, India. It originates in the Chote Nagpur plateau, then passes east through Netarhat in Latehar district. It merges northwest from Hydernagar with the Son River, itself a tributary of the Ganges River.

sakaree
